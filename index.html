Most Popular Marian Apparitions

Below are a list of the most commonly known Marian Apparitions around the world.
This is by no means an exhaustive list as there have been hundreds of apparitions throughout history.
Click on each section below to learn more.

Our Lady of Guadalupe

Mary proclaimed herself "the Mother of the true God who gives life" and left her image permanently upon the tilma of Juan Diego, a recent convert to Christianity. Through this apparition of Mary, all of Mexico converted to Christianity.

Learn More

Our Lady of Laus

Benoite Rencurel, a poor shepherdess, was born in 1647. The Virgin Mary started appearing to her in 1664 and continued visiting her throughout the rest of her life. The Blessed Mother told her to “pray continuously for sinners.”

Learn More

Our Lady of The Miraculous Medal

Mary appears to St. Catherine Labouré at the Rue de Bac Chapel. Standing on a globe with lights streaming from her hands. The apparition had the following inscription: “O Mary, conceived without sin, pray for us who have recourse to thee.”

Learn More

Our Lady of La Salette

Six thousand feet up in the French Alps, Mary is believed to have come to Maximin and Melanie while they tended sheep. Her appearance in sorrow and tears called for conversion and penance for sins. La Salette's prophetic message can be applied to what our world is living through today.

Learn More

Our Lady of Lourdes

Mary appears to young Bernadett in the grotto of Massabielle while gathering firewood with her sister and friend. Mary asks her to pray for the conversion of sinners, as well as to tell the priests to build a chapel there. The water from the spring at Lourdes has miraculously healed many people of infirmities.

Learn More

Our Lady of Fátima

While tending sheep, Lucia de Santos and her two cousins, Francisco and Jacinta Marto, reported six apparitions of Mary, who identified herself as "Our Lady of the Rosary." Mary urged prayer of the rosary, penance for the conversion of sinners and consecration of Russia to her Immaculate Heart.

Learn More

Totus Tuus

"I belong entirely to you, and all that I have is yours. I take you for my all. O Mary, give me your heart"
